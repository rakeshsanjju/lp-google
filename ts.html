Risks of Ignoring Vein Problems

  • Painful leg ulcers that take time to heal.
  • Severe cases can lead to tissue damage or amputation.
  • Persistent skin darkening and swelling around the ankles.
  • In advanced conditions, neglected veins may progress to deep vein thrombosis or even gangrene.
  • Early diagnosis and timely treatment can prevent these complications.

Common Causes of Varicose Veins

  • Family history or genetic weakness in vein walls and valves.
  • Standing or sitting for prolonged hours, reducing blood circulation in the legs.
  • Pregnancy-related hormonal and pressure changes that strain leg veins.
  • Obesity and lack of regular physical activity increasing venous pressure.
  • Hormonal changes or aging that weaken vein elasticity and valve function.

What is Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are twisted, swollen veins in the legs, located just beneath the skin. They may appear brownish, blue, or purple in colour. If the valves are weak or damaged, blood can pool up and increase pressure in the veins. This excess pressure causes the veins to widen and expand, leading them to form varicose veins. They are more common in the legs because standing and walking increases pressure in the lower body veins; as for women, older adults and people with a genetic history are at a higher risk of suffering from varicose veins.

Common Symptoms

  • Visible blue, red, or purple veins on the legs
  • Pain or cramps after standing or sitting too long.
  • Heaviness, burning, or swelling in the legs
  • Skin changes, radiating pain, or occasional bleeding

Minimally Invasive Treatments

  • Endovenous Laser Treatment (EVLT)
  • Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)
  • Sclerotherapy and CLaCs for smaller veins.
  • VenaSeal (Medical Superglue Treatment)

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes varicose veins?

+

Varicose veins occur when the valves in your veins weaken or get damaged, causing blood to pool and veins to swell.

What are the symptoms of varicose veins?

+

Visible bulging veins, leg pain, heaviness, swelling, cramps, burning sensation, and skin discoloration.

Are varicose veins dangerous?

+

Most are not life-threatening, but if untreated, they may lead to ulcers, bleeding, or blood clots.

How are varicose veins treated?

+

Modern options include Endovenous Laser Treatment (EVLT), Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA), VenaSeal, sclerotherapy, and other minimally invasive procedures.

What is the latest and most advanced treatment for varicose veins?

+

EVLT, RFA, and VenaSeal™ are considered the most advanced, minimally invasive procedures with fast recovery and no major scarring.

Is laser treatment better than traditional surgery?

+

Yes. Laser treatment is safer, less painful, scar-free, and allows patients to return to normal activities within 1–2 days compared to surgery.

Is varicose vein treatment painful?

+

No. Advanced treatments like Laser, RFA, and VenaSeal™ are nearly painless, done under local anesthesia, and require no overnight hospital stay.

How long does recovery take after advanced treatment?

+

Most patients can walk immediately and return to daily activities within 1–2 days.
